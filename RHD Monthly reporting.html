    Section I: Antenatal Care (ANC)

    1. Percentage of mothers with at least 5 antenatal visits in a cohort

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Number of women with 5 ANC visits and above in a cohort expressed as a percentage in a cohort.
    Numerator: Number of mothers with at least 5 antenatal visits in a cohort
    Denominator: Total number of pregnant women in a cohort
    Indicator Rationale: This indicator is a measure of women's encounter with the health personnel. It is assumed that the targeted visits would give the women an opportunity to access a full package of ANC interventions. The visits would also assist the health providers to identify complications on time and address them.




    2. Percentage of mothers receiving at least 2 TTV doses in a cohort.

    Denominator: Total number of pregnant women in a cohort
    Indicator Rationale: This indicator is a measure of women's encounter with the health personnel. The visits would also assist the health providers to identify complications on time and address them.




    3. Percentage of women undergoing pregnancy test in a cohort

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Number of women that have tested for pregnancy expressed as a percentage based on total number in a cohort.
    Numerator: Number of women undergoing pregnancy test in the first trimester in a cohort.
    Denominator: Total number of pregnant women in a cohort
    Indicator Rationale: Testing women for pregnancy helps to enroll women who have not yet showed signs of pregnancy physically in antenatal. This test would increase ANC enrollment in first trimester.




    4. Percentage of women undergoing syphilis test in a cohort

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: women undergoing syphilis test expressed as a percentage in a cohort.
    Numerator: Number of women that have received syphilis test in a cohort
    Denominator: Total number of pregnant women in a cohort
    Indicator Rationale: Tracking this indicator is important as it informs decisions on quality of care at a point of care in a facility.




    5. Percentage of mothers receiving atleast 3 doses of SP in a cohort

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Total number of mothers receiving 3 or more doses of SP expressed as a percentage in a cohort.
    Numerator: Number of mothers with at least 5 antenatal visits in a cohort.

    Indicator Rationale: As required by MoH policy , all pregnant women should receive at least 3 doses of sulphadoxine-pyrimethamine (SP) for Malaria prevention.




    Section II: Maternity

    6. Institutional Maternal Mortality Ratio (per 100,000 deliveries in a facility)

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Institutional Maternal Mortality Ratio (per 100,000 deliveries in a facility) is a Per 100,000 indicator, with the numerator described as Number of Maternal deaths in health facilities and denominator described as Number of deliveries in health facilities.
    Numerator: Number of Maternal deaths in a health facility.
    Denominator: Number of deliveries in health facilities. NB:
    Denominator: ( Maternity Monthly Report (“RHD MAT Survival/Survival Alive not HIV exp” + “RHD MAT Survival/Survival Alive Exp No NVP” + “RHD MAT Survival/Survival Alive NVP Started” ” + “RHD MAT Survival/Survival Alive unknown Exp” + “RHD MAT Survival/Survival Alive Neonatal death”) OR
    Denominator: HMIS 15 (“HMIS Total # of Live births”) + HMIS 17 (“HMIS 17 Live Births”) (Note: This data is also available through MDSR, IDSR, and the Maternal and Neonatal Death Report. Data should be triangulated on a regular basis)).
    Indicator Rationale: Maternal death is a direct measure of quality of care. Analysis of this data can inform health personnel on critical areas that need to be addressed. Maternal death audit reports provide reliable data to track maternal deaths monthly.
    Indicator Rationale: This indicator is a measure of women's encounter with the health personnel. It is assumed that the targeted visits would give the women an opportunity to access a full package of ANC interventions. The visits would also assist the health providers to identify complications on time and address them.




    7. Institutional Neonatal Mortality Rate (per 1000)

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Number of deaths during the first 28 completed days of life per 1000 live births, as reported in HMIS, in each period.
    Numerator: Number of infants who died during the first 28 days of life in health facilities.
    Denominator: Total number of live births recorded in the same period in health facilities (
    Denominator: Maternity Clinic Monthly Reporting Form (“RHD MAT Newborn Survival/PMTCT Alive not HIV Exp + RHD MAT Newborn Survival/PMTCT Alive Exp no NVP + RHD MAT Newborn Survival/PMTCT Alive NVP Started + RHD MAT Newborn Survival/PMTCT Alive Unknown Exp + RHD MAT Newborn Survival/PMTCT Alive Neonatal Death”) Or
    Denominator: HMIS 15 (“HMIS Total # of Live birth”) + HMIS 17 Monthly Reporting Form (“HMIS 17 Live birth”) ).
    Indicator Rationale: Mortality during the neonatal period accounts for a large proportion of child deaths. Some can be prevented by effective antenatal, delivery and postnatal care to women and proper care to newborns. This indicator measures the quality of these services at the facility level.




    8. Percentage of babies started on breastfeeding within 60 minutes of birth

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Number of babies starting breast feeding within 60mins of birth expressed as a percentage of total number of babies.
    Numerator: Number of babies Breastfeeding Initiated within 60 mins.
    Denominator: Total number of babies born in that facility in that month.
    Indicator Rationale: Early initiation of breastfeeding within 60mins ensures that the infant receives colostrum, or “first milk”, which is rich in protective factors.




    9. Fresh stillbirth per 1,000 deliveries in health facilities

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Fresh stillbirth per 1,000 deliveries in health facilities is a Per 1000 indicator, with the numerator described as Number of fresh stillbirths and denominator described as Number of deliveries in health facilities.
    Numerator: Number of Fresh still births in a facility.
    Denominator: Number of deliveries in health facilities.
    Indicator Rationale: Fresh still birth is a direct measure of ANC and intrapartum care. Some can be prevented by effective antenatal and delivery services.




    10. Percentage of mothers who had (pre)Eclampsia (Ecl) who received anticonvulsants in a facility in that month.

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Mothers who received anticonvulsants as a percentage of pre-eclampsia and eclampsia.
    Numerator: Number of Mothers who received anticonvulsants in a month.
    Denominator: Number of women diagnosed with (Pre-) Eclampsia
    Indicator Rationale: This indicator measures the quality of care for women with Hypertensive disorders. At the same time it can inform the availability and use of anticonvulsants at facility level.




    11. Percentage of Mothers receiving antenatal corticosteroids in a facility per month

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Mothers receiving antenatal corticosteroids as a percentage of prematurity newborn complications.
    Numerator: Mothers receiving antenatal corticosteroids.
    Denominator: Total number of newborn prematurity complications.
    Indicator Rationale: Prematurity is one major contributing factor to Neonatal deaths. Administration of antenatal corticosteroids improves the survival rate of premature babies




    12. Percentage of babies with asphyxia successfully resuscitated

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Number of successfully resuscitated asphyxiated babies as percentage based on total number of birth asphyxia.
    Numerator: Number of asphyxiated babies successfully resuscitated (by drying and wrapping, clearing air way and stimulation, bag and mask ventilation)
    Denominator: Total number of asphyxiated babies according to HBB report form.
    Indicator Rationale: Asphyxia is one major contributing factor of Neonatal deaths. Measuring this indicator provide insights into the quality of resuscitation interventions.




    13. Percentage of babies receiving tetracycline eye ointment(TEO) for all live births

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Babies receiving TEO expressed as a percentage based on total number of live births.
    Numerator: Number of babies receiving TEO
    Denominator: Total number of live births.
    Indicator Rationale: Neonates are prone to infections such as eye infections. Tetracycline application is vital in preventing eye infections. This indicator provides insights on the quality of routine newborn care and the usage of TEO at facility level.




    14. Percentage of deliveries by skilled birth attendants

    Item Description
    Indicator Definition: Number of deliveries by skilled birth attendants expressed as a percentage of total number of deliveries.
    Numerator: Number of deliveries by skilled birth attendants.
    Denominator: Total number of deliveries.
    Indicator Rationale: Complications during pregnancy and childbirth are a leading cause of death and disability among women of reproductive age in Malawi. Access to skilled care during childbirth is a key strategy to reduce both maternal and neonatal deaths. Maternal mortality itself can be very difficult to measure, making it critical to track related indicators.
